## What is oatmeal?

Oatmeal is a nutritious breakfast food made from oats that have been ground, steel-cut, or rolled. It's a versatile whole grain that provides sustained energy and numerous health benefits.

## Types of oatmeal

### Steel-cut oats
- Also known as Irish oats
- Chopped into small pieces with steel blades
- Takes 15-20 minutes to cook
- Has a chewy, nutty texture

### Rolled oats
- Steamed and flattened with rollers
- Cooks in 5-10 minutes
- Softer texture than steel-cut
- Most common type for breakfast

### Quick oats
- Rolled oats cut into smaller pieces
- Cooks in 1-2 minutes
- Softer, mushier texture
- Good for baking and quick meals

### Instant oats
- Pre-cooked and dried
- Just add hot water
- Very soft texture
- Often contains added flavors and sugar

Key benefits include:
- **High in fiber**: Supports digestive health and helps you feel full
- **Beta-glucan**: A type of soluble fiber that may help lower cholesterol
- **Protein**: Contains all essential amino acids
- **Vitamins and minerals**: Good source of manganese, phosphorus, magnesium, and iron

## Basic preparation

### Stovetop method

```bash
# For 1 serving
1/2 cup rolled oats
1 cup water or milk
Pinch of salt
```

1. Bring water/milk to a boil in a saucepan
2. Add oats and salt
3. Reduce heat and simmer for 5-10 minutes, stirring occasionally
4. Remove from heat when desired consistency is reached

### Microwave method

1. Combine oats, liquid, and salt in a microwave-safe bowl
2. Microwave on high for 2-3 minutes
3. Stir and let cool for a minute before eating

## Toppings and variations

Popular additions:
- **Fresh fruits**: Berries, bananas, apples
- **Nuts and seeds**: Almonds, walnuts, chia seeds, flax seeds
- **Sweeteners**: Honey, maple syrup, brown sugar
- **Spices**: Cinnamon, vanilla, nutmeg
- **Protein**: Greek yogurt, protein powder, nut butter

## Storage tips

- Store dry oats in an airtight container for up to 2 years
- Cooked oatmeal keeps in the refrigerator for 3-5 days
- Freeze cooked oatmeal in portions for up to 3 months
- Reheat with a splash of milk or water to restore creaminess

<Note>
For best results, use a 1:2 ratio of oats to liquid, but adjust based on your preferred consistency.
</Note>
